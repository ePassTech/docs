---
title: "Registration numbers"
description: "How premises registration numbers work in ePass"
---

Every premises registered in ePass is assigned a unique **10-digit registration number**. This number is generated automatically by the system when a premises is created. You do not need to enter one yourself.

## Format

Registration numbers are always exactly 10 digits long, for example:

```
1048230917
```

Numbers may include leading zeros, so the full 10-digit value should always be used when referencing a premises.

## Where registration numbers appear

- On the premises detail page within your account
- On licence certificates issued after approval
- In the [public register](https://app.epass.service.gov.scot), where anyone can search for registered premises
- In QR codes linked to your premises

## Standardisation

As of March 2026, all registration numbers follow a consistent 10-digit format. Existing registration numbers were automatically converted, so no action was needed. If you previously noted a shorter registration number for your premises, use the updated 10-digit version going forward.

## Things to know

- Registration numbers are **unique**. No two premises share the same number.
- Numbers are assigned by the system and **cannot be changed** manually.
- When a new premises is added through a change request, a registration number is generated automatically.
